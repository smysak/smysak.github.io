Q4 Rhetorical Analysis Assignment


One important skill to have, is the ability to analyze what we call the rhetorical situation.

This refers to the purpose, audience, topic, writer, and context of a piece of writing.

Understanding how these variables have affected the text, how it was written, and how it was received, is important for understanding the piece as a whole.


For this assignment, you will study a piece of writing by Richard Louv. Specifically, you will read an excerpt from the introduction to his book, The Last Child in the Woods.

You will then write a 750 word essay, commenting on the text.

Click here to view the text in .pdf format.


Is Louv simply trying to sell his book? Did the publisher ask him to write an introduction to spark the reader's interest?

Certainly that is a part of the rhetorical situation, but hardly worth commenting on.

The important question you should ask is, what was in the writer's heart as he composed the piece?


Ensure that as you write, you reflect on the following aspects of the rhetorical situation:

  • the author's purpose
  • the book's intended audience
  • the broad topic that is being discussed in the writing
  • who the author is
  • the broader context (i.e. what is going on in the wider world, and how the text relates to the movements of history)
